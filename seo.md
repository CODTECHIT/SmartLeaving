## 0. Important compliance note (read before publishing anything)

Before writing "foreign investors can buy land in India" content, keep this legal boundary in every page:

| Buyer type | Residential/Commercial property | Agricultural/Farm land |
|---|---|---|
| NRI / OCI | ✅ Allowed, no RBI approval needed | ❌ Not allowed (only via inheritance/gift) |
| Foreign national (non-Indian origin, non-resident) | ❌ Needs RBI approval | ❌ Not allowed |
| Foreign national living in India on work/business visa (FEMA resident) | ✅ Generally allowed like a resident | ❌ Still restricted in most cases |

**Practical rule for content:** Market "Farmlands" only to resident Indian buyers or NRIs looking at post-inheritance/gift scenarios — never as a direct-purchase product for foreign nationals. For NRIs/foreigners, lead with **Commercial Lands, Industrial Lands, Open Plots (non-agri), Villa Plots, and Apartments**. Add a short disclaimer line on every NRI-facing page: *"Agricultural land purchase is subject to FEMA restrictions — please consult our advisory team for eligible property types."* This protects you legally and actually builds trust (real investors respect a company that states rules upfront instead of overselling).

### Two different legal routes — keep these separate in content

| Route | Who it applies to | What it allows |
|---|---|---|
| **FEMA individual property rules** | NRI, OCI, foreign individual buying personally | Residential + commercial property allowed (no agri land) |
| **FDI policy (DPIIT/RBI automatic route)** | Foreign company setting up an Indian entity/subsidiary | Can acquire **industrial land** to build factories, pharma units, warehouses, logistics parks — most manufacturing sectors allow 100% FDI under automatic route, no prior government approval needed |

This second route is actually your **strongest angle for foreign investors** — it's a cleaner, government-encouraged process (unlike individual property buying which is full of restrictions). Since your site already has Industrial Services (Pharma projects, Genome Valley-type deals, ₹500 Cr private funding), this should be marketed as a distinct, equally prominent pillar — not folded into "real estate for NRIs."
